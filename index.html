Ted Livingston

Kik

CEO

Ted Livingston

CEO | Kik

Ted Livingston is the founder and CEO of Kik, a chat network with more than 240 million registered users and 40 percent of U.S. teens. Founded in 2009, Kik is headquartered in Waterloo, Ontario, and has raised $120.5 million from investors including Tencent and Union Square Ventures.

Ted also maintains an active interest in the University of Waterloo’s Velocity Fund, a startup accelerator he conceived of and first funded.

Teds's Talk: Fireside Chat with Daryna Kulya

Communication through messaging apps have become a societal norm and shaped the way we interact with individuals. Canadian bred, Kik Messenger with over 270M users is one of the top players in this high-growth space and has disrupted the future of messaging through bots, new communication media types and unparalleled security. Join Ted Livingston (CEO, Kik Messenger) alongside Daryna Kulya (Founder of Product Hunt Toronto) as they unravel Ted’s entrepreneurial journey, predict the future of messaging and how Kik stays competitive with behemoths such as Facebook. Daryna and Ted will also provide advice to the audience on how to take on a non-traditional career path, experiment while developing your career and stay relevant in our rapidly progressing technology industry.

Matt Wallaert

Microsoft

Behavioral Scientist

Matt Wallaert

Behavioral Scientist | Microsoft

Matt Wallaert is a behavioral scientist working at the intersection of technology and human behavior. After several years in academia and two successful startup exits, he joined Microsoft, where he works across a variety of products and projects to design technology that helps people live happier, healthier lives, as well as the East Coast Lead for Microsoft Ventures, managing the company's relationships with prominent VCs and startups.

Wallaert maintains an active research program and is a frequent speaker on the science of behavior change and product design. He sits on the board a variety of startups and non-profits, and cultivates pro-social side projects like GetRaised.com, which has helped underpaid women ask for and earn millions of dollars in salary increases.

Matt's Talk: Matt Wallaert is a white guy. A cowboy boot-wearing, born in the country, horse riding white guy. He is also a prominent male advocate and the scientist behind GetRaised, a free tool that has helped women earn over $2.3B in raises. In this session, Matt will talk about his continuing journey as a male advocate and how men in general can transform how they approach the issue of gender equity. Full of practical examples, the focus here is on the applied: this talk is about going beyond the moral imperative of equity to the daily living of an advocate.

FAQ

Is a schedule available?

No. The schedule will be posted once the speakers have been finalized.

What am I getting for my $$$?

We’ve been working hard to cut prices as much as possible to make sure cost doesn’t get in your way. Early Bird Tickets are $30 and Regular Tickets are $50. You get access to keynotes, workshops, challenges, the Tech Expo, our pub night social, and a delicious breakfast, lunch and dinner!

What’s the difference between CUTC and say, a hackathon?

At CUTC, it’s not just about hacking. Sure, you can get your hands dirty and create things during our events like the Microsoft Machine Learning Challenge or CGI’s Imagine Challenge; however, you also get to learn from the people in the trenches--entrepreneurs, pioneers in technology and the guys who build and maintain the tech we use everyday.

Will there be conference subsidies?

We'll be realeasing more information very soon. Watch out on our Facebook and Twitter for more updates!

When and where is CUTC Elevate?

CUTC will be held on May 13-14th at the UofT O.I.S.E. building located at 252 Bloor Street West, Toronto.

Where can I stay during the conference?

Need a place to stay for the weekend? Try AirBnB, CouchSurfers or crashing at a friends place!

I still have questions!

Send 'em our way by shooting us a message on our Facebook page or email us at support@cutc.ca!

I'm coming from outside Toronto - how do I get there?

You can arrange transportation with a friend, Uber driver, or travel to the conference by train!

Can I transfer my ticket to another person?

Absolutely, just send us a message on our Facebook page with the details and we’ll send that person a link to fill out a new registration form so they don’t have to use your name tag.

I’m interested in volunteering for CUTC, how can I get involved?

Applications for volunteers will be released in late March. Watch out on our Facebook and Twitter for more updates!
